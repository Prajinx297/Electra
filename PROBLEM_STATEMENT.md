# Problem Statement Alignment

Electra addresses democratic participation barriers for India's eligible voters by reducing confusion, improving confidence, and guiding users through civic steps with adaptive support.

## Civic Confusion

Voters often know they should participate but do not know the next concrete step. Electra maps each question to a typed journey state and renders the exact module needed, such as registration checks, deadline calculators, polling guidance, or summaries.

## Language And Reading Level

A single explanation style excludes first-time voters, elderly users, and people who prefer plain language. Electra uses cognitive levels to adapt responses into simple, detailed, or legal explanations. This turns technical election process language into guidance that matches the user's comfort level.

## Drop-Off During Multi-Step Journeys

Election tasks are rarely one-screen tasks. Electra stores journey history and supports temporal rewind so users can recover from mistaken choices without restarting. The civic outcome is lower abandonment and better completion.

## Invisible Confusion

Most systems only know when users click submit. Electra tracks passive confusion signals such as rereads, retreats, timeouts, and stuck states. These analytics create a heatmap that shows where voters need clearer guidance.

## Trust And Official Grounding

Users need confidence that election guidance is not invented or partisan. Electra routes Oracle responses through typed schemas, uses official-source trust metadata, and validates render instructions before UI changes. The technical decision produces a civic outcome: safer guidance and clearer provenance.

## Election Integrity Education

Election systems can feel opaque. Electra includes an election simulator with ballot ingestion, tallying, certification, anomaly injection, and audit flows. Users can see how custody, recount risk, and certification work before they encounter real-world election claims.

## Google Solution Challenge Fit

Electra uses Gemini for adaptive reasoning, Firebase Auth for low-friction access, Firestore for journey continuity, Firebase Analytics for civic UX measurement, and Google Cloud deployment patterns for scale. Each service directly supports the problem: making democratic participation easier, safer, and more understandable.
